TADAO ANDO

BIOGRAPHY

Tadao Ando, one of the most renowned contemporary Japanese architects and a leading figure in international architecture and design,
was born on September 13, 1941 in Osaka, Japan. He is the 18th Pritzker award winner and the third Japanese architect to receive the international honor.

Tadao Andos'style is famously known as Critical Regionalism. Utilizing natural elements, like sun, rain, and wind is his distinctive way to present
aesthetics and his favorite medium to work with is silky smooth concrete which people also call it "Ando's concrete". "In all my works, light is
an important controlling factor," says Ando. "I create enclosed spaces mainly by means of thick concrete walls. The primary reason is to create
a place for the individual, a zone for oneself within society. When the external factors of a city's environment require the wall to be
without openings, the interior must be especially full and satisfying."

One special thing about Ando is that he has never attended a traditional course on Architecture. Lacking a degree in architecture and training
with a master architect, Ando attributes his achievement to self-directed study of buildings in Europe and the United States.
